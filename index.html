How a Master's Education in Kinesiology led to UX/UI.

My UX/UI Journey

There were days where I would lay down and wonder “what now?” Following my Master’s in Kinesiology with an emphasis in Sport and Excercise Psychology, the job market seemed bleak and hopeless. COVID-19 had taken a turn for the worst, diminishing my hopes of finishing certification to become a mental performance consultant. So you might ask, “how did you end up here?” That question in itself, speaks about my personality. A young individual who is comfortable with the uncomfortable. An ambitious individual always looking for ways to adapt, challenge oneself and learn. I began my UX career due to unmitigated circumstances but the choice to pursue this career remained true to how I’ve always been: someone wanting to provide quality service to those in need.
I believe that effective product designing comes from the stories our users tell. Stories about happiness, frustrations, and wishful thoughts all fuel the designing process I undertake with each design. By using various individuals' wishful thoughts and ideas, I am able to design products that provide quality user face interactions.
